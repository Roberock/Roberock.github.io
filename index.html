Project

Currently I am a postdoctoral Research Scholar involved in a collaborative research effort with the National Institute of Aerospace (NIA) and the Dynamic Systems and Control Branch at NASA Langley. Engineering project and missions often require innovative systems which are designed specifically to operate in extreme environments under strongly varying operating conditions. Uncertainty is inevitably affecting the performance of those systems and is typically described as aleatory, which is uncertainty due to inherent variability and randomness in the sytem and environment, or epistemic uncertainty, which is uncertainty due to lack of knowledge. In many cases, experimental data is extremely costly and therefore limited, making uncertainty difficult to definitively quantify. This research project intends to develop mathematical frameworks for determining the effects of uncertainty in the predictions of computational model of safety-critical systems. One of the goals is to develop a mathematical framework for distributionally robust reliability-based desing-optimization capable of prescribing optimized systems desings, enanched with respect to the relevant sources of aleatory and epistemic uncertainty.

Research Areas & Expertise

My research primarily focused on the development of methods for analysis and optimization of complex systems and critical infrastructures under uncertainty. I am interested in probabilistic and non-probabilistic frameworks for the quantification of aleatory and epistemic uncertainty, in the modelling of networked infrastructures and in the simulation, reliability assessment and design optimization of those complex systems. I activly contributed to the development of resilience/reliability/vulnerability assessment frameworks for power grid systems. In doing so, I had the chance to work with various machine learning techiniques ( such as Reinforcement learning, Artifical Neural Networks, Bayesian Model updating, Gaussian Process and Clustering and Supervised/Unsupervised Anomalies Detections)
