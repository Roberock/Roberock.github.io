Project

I am currently workoing as postdoc on smart maintenance solutions and develoment of tools to achieve the golas of the industry 4.0 vision. The project I am invoved in is called DAYTIME and is a collaborative project between Philips Electronics and the Technical university of Eindhoven. Prior joining the project I worked at the National Institute of Aerospace in collaboration with the Dynamic Systems and Control Branch at NASA Langley. Engineering project and missions often require innovative systems which are designed specifically to operate in extreme environments under strongly varying operating conditions. Uncertainty is inevitably affecting the performance of those systems and is typically described as aleatory, which is uncertainty due to inherent variability and randomness in the sytem and environment, or epistemic uncertainty, which is uncertainty due to lack of knowledge. In many cases, experimental data is extremely costly and therefore limited, making uncertainty difficult to definitively quantify. This research project intends to develop mathematical frameworks for determining the effects of uncertainty in the predictions of computational model of safety-critical systems. One of the goals is to develop a mathematical framework for distributionally robust reliability-based desing-optimization capable of prescribing optimized systems desings, enanched with respect to the relevant sources of aleatory and epistemic uncertainty.
