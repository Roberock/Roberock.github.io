Profile

Currently I am a postdoctoral Research Scholar involved in a collaborative research effort with the National Institute of Aerospace (NIA) and the Dynamic Systems and Control Branch at NASA Langley. My research interests primarily focus on the development of methods for distributionally robust reliance/reliability-based design optimization, generalized uncertainty quantification, and stochastic modelling of complex systems and critical infrastructures.

Academic Background

I received my PhD from the University of Liverpool while doing research at the Institute for Risk and Uncertainty + School of Engineering. My dissertation focused on developing robust computational frameworks for the resilience, vulnerability and reliability analysis of power grids and other interconnected safety-critical systems. I obtained my second Masters in Decision Making Under Risk and Uncertainty (Liverpool University) and my first Master in Enrgy Engineering (University of Bologna).
